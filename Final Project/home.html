Picos de Europa

The Picos de Europa National Park is located in the Cantabrian Mountains, between the provinces of Asturias, León and Cantabria. Its vast expanse comprises territories belonging to the boroughs of Amieva, Cangas de Onís, Onís, Cabrales, Peñamellera Alta and Peñamellera Baja.

A great variety of different landscapes come together in this setting which are home to a peculiar animal kingdom of singular importance in the whole of the Iberian Peninsula. It is common to observe the flight of the golden eagle dominating the mountain; vultures and owls nesting on the steep walls of the mountains; capercaillie in wooded areas and mallards fishing in the Lakes of Covadonga, alongside the coots in Lake Ercina and the alpine choughs that find food under the protection of visitors.
